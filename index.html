A Guide to Shuuen no Shiori

by @ALTCODE255

Introduction

The Bookmark of Demise Project (終焉ノ栞プロジェクト, Shuuen no Shiori Purojekuto) (May 12th, 2012 – January 27th, 2016), colloquially known as Shuuenpro, is a multimedia Vocaloid series created by:

  • 150P (music)
  • Suzumu (スズム) (story & lyrics)
  • komine (こみね) (illustration)
  • Saine (さいね) (illustration & character design)

that is composed of five albums created by 150P & Suzumu, four light novels written by Suzumu, a 33-chapter manga illustrated by Amino Yuki, and an eight-track voice drama featuring various voice actors. Seven songs are accompanied with official MVs.

Story

The story of Shuuen no Shiori can primarily be classified as horror and mystery. Major elements of gore, death, and unreality are present. Other content warnings may be found on the series' DoesTheDogDie page (contributions from fans appreciated).

On the normal, unchanging campus, it suddenly occurred; A murder case where a body was cut open, right into two. On the other side of the unsolved case, the rumor-loving high school students became involved in a certain ritual. From the extremely popular songs on Nico Nico Douga, begins the Project.

What is the "Bookmark of Demise"...?
Who is the traitor...?
What will the conclusion to this story be...?

So, can you solve this mystery?

The beginning of the story. The opening act.

Outside of the window was a cloudy sky. The season in which summer has not yet started. There existed a single rumor whispered about.
No one knows the details. No, no one was supposed to know them.
Just that, even if the empty book and bookmark are found, it is said that they must never be touched.

—For it is the Bookmark of Demise.

Incompletion

On October 10th, 2015, Suzumu confessed to claiming ghostwritten songs as his own and earning dishonest profit from them, and announced that he would be retiring from all Vocaloid-related activities. (Translation here.)

The full extent of Suzumu's plagiarism is unknown. Additionally, because Suzumu had written/arranged songs outside of Shuuen no Shiori, we do not know if it extended to his involvement with the series.

Since the incident, both 150P and komine seem to have gone completely inactive without notice. Consequently, although the manga got its own original ending in January of 2016, the project itself has never received a proper conclusion (and more than likely never will).

You are welcome to get into the series in spite of this, but be warned; there are countless mysteries unsolved and many loose ends untied.
